Before the Ceremony

मेंहदी (Mehndi)

Mehndi, or henna, is applied to the hands and feet of the bride and her female friends and family. It is believed that the deeper the color on the bride's hands, the stronger the love between husband and wife.

पीठी (Pithi)

Pithi is a golden paste made of chick pea flour, turmeric, sandalwood, and other ingredients. This paste is applied to the face, arms, and legs of the bride and groom before the wedding to signify good health and leave their skin glowing.

The Ceremony

The Hindu wedding ceremony is elaborate, with every step rooted in vedic tradition, signifying various aspects of life that are to follow after the wedding. The ceremony takes place inside a mandap, or stage, which is decorated with flowers.

द्वार पूजा (Dwar Puja) - The Groom's Arrival

The groom arrives for the wedding with his family. They are all greeted by the bride's family. The bride's mother then performs a welcoming ritual for good luck and leads the groom to the mandap.

कन्या आगमन (Kanya Aagaman) - The Bride's Arrival

Upon arrival, the bride's father escorts her into the mandap.

गणेश पूजा (Ganesh Puja) - Worshipping Lord Ganesha

The ceremony begins by offering a prayer to Lord Ganesha. Lord Ganesha is worshipped, so he may remove all obstacles and bless the couple.

जय माला (Jai Mala) - Exchanging of Garlands

The couple exchange flower garlands, signifying the acceptance of one another and to pledge their respect for one another as partners in life.

कन्यादान (Kanya Daan) & हस्त मिलाप (Hasta Milap) - Giving Away The Bride

In the Hindu religion, the Kanya Daan is considered the most magnificent offering a bride's parents make. The Kanya Daan symbolizes the bride in the form of Goddess Laxmi and groom as Lord Narayana. Here, the bride's family displays the act of giving her to the groom and his family.

सप्तपदी (Saptapadi) - The Seven Steps

Saptapadi is the most important rite of a Hindu marriage ceremony. After tying together their wedding scarves, the couple takes seven steps around the holy fire. After the seventh step, the couple legally become husband and wife.

Each step around the fire represents a different vow:

  • To nourish each other
  • To remain lifelong friends
  • To be together forever
  • To care for our children
  • To share our joys and sorrows
  • To preserve our wealth
  • To grow together in strength
